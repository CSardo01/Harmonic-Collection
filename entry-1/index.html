But not those windows

These Windows! The ones not built by Microsoft.

At first, the operating system and the architectural element seem like separate entities. They are in fact more closely related, both offering glimpses into alternate spaces.

Similarly, a building without windows gives off a sorta MS-DOS vibe.

While both the building and MS-DOS have a certain aesthetic, They seem inaccessible to the general public. In fact, 33 Thomas street was the AT&T Long Lines building, however, it has also been speculated to be the headquarters of an NSA mass surveillance hub. Ironically the MS-DOS/Terminal aesthetic is often used to represent hacking in popular culture.

Why windows?

Truthfully it was one of the first things that popped into my mind. However, upon further contemplation, I realized that there's alot to unpack. There's the physicality of windows and what they're made of. For example how reflective the pane of glass is. However, there is also the more metaphorical aspect of windows and how they make us feel psychological.
